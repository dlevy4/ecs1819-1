Marvin Miller


Influencers

Curt Flood

Curt Flood opposed the Reserve Clause in baseball in a lawsuit with the help of Marvin Miller. This is commonly known as the event that started Miller's baseball career.

United Steelworkers

Miller was the Economic Advisor and Vice President for the United Steelworkers. He commonly negotiated contracts for them, and this helped him become the negotiator that he was.

Bowie Kuhn

Bowie Kuhn was the commissioner of baseball from 1969-1984. The Supreme Court case Flood v. Kuhn, ultamately gave MLB players Free agency, despite what Kuhn fought for.



In the 20 Years preceding Miller, There was a Major League Baseball Players Association, but it rarely got anything done. It was started by baseball's owners in 1949 to give the illusion of a union, to try to keep the players happy. The only important thing that the MLBPA got done were pension and benifits plans.

During that time, Marvin Miller was working for the United Steelworkers. Miller was the Vice President and Chief Negotiator, where he was in charce of arguing and writing most of their collective bargaining agreememnts. This work in labor rights proved to be important. In 1966, Miller toured spring training camps to talk to players, and tell them about what a collective barganing agreement could do for them. In 1968, Miller was elected Executive director of the MLBPA in a vote of 489-136.

In 1968 Marvin miller, Executive Director of the Major League Baseball Players Association, negotiated for the first Collective Bargaining Agreement. This is an Agreement between all owners and players. It involved a minimum wage increase from $6,000 to $10,000, daily expense increases, brought arbitration for player grievances, and a much better pension plan.
